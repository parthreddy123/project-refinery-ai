Project Refinery AI

12 working AI prototypes for refinery and trading operations, trained on real process data and connected to live market prices.

Total addressable value: $358M - $964M / year

01Mission

Build 12 working AI prototypes for Reliance R&M refinery and trading operations — trained on real process data, connected to live market prices — as the Strategy & AI Transformation team.

Working models on real data, not assessments on slides.

Reliance R&M operates the world's largest refining complex at Jamnagar (1.24M bpd across DTA + SEZ). A systems assessment identified 5 structural problems and sized the AI opportunity at $964M – $2.7B/year. Project Refinery AI is the execution of that assessment.

02The 5 Problems

Structural gaps identified across the Jamnagar complex that AI can close.

Problem 1

Islands of Automation

Best-in-class point solutions (Aspen, Honeywell, SAP, OSIsoft) that don't talk to each other.

Problem 2

No Unified Data Platform

Everything converges in SAP BW or Excel. No single source of truth for operational data.

Problem 3

APC Underperformance

Controllers running at less than 70% uptime, losing approximately $4-5M/yr per 1% downtime.

Problem 4

Planning-Execution Disconnect

PIMS weekly plan to Excel to manual coordination to RTO offline. No closed loop.

Problem 5

Trading Flying Blind

Overnight batch VaR, manual market intelligence, no AI signals on $50B+ crude procurement.

03The 12 Projects

Each prototype targets a specific operational gap with real data and measurable value.

P1

DWSIM CLI Engine

Extract the open-source DWSIM process simulator (.NET) from its GUI and wrap it as a CLI/Python API. Enables conversational AI to run thermodynamic simulations on demand.

FeedsP2, P5, P6, P10
Depends onNone (foundation)
DataDWSIM source from GitHub
ValueFoundation layer
P2

Crude Selection Optimizer

AI that recommends the optimal crude slate given current market prices and refinery yield predictions. Even 0.5% improvement in crude selection on $50B+ procurement = $250M/year.

FeedsP12
Depends onP1

Value$68 - $181M / year

Predict product quality (e.g., butane content) from live process sensors, eliminating the 2-4 hour wait for lab results and reducing quality giveaway.

P5

Blend Optimizer

Optimize product blending (gasoline, diesel) to hit spec targets (octane, sulfur, pour point) at minimum component cost. Targets quality giveaway reduction from 1-3% to under 0.5%.

Tennessee Eastman Process

input/tep/
For P3, P7, P10 — ~150MB

GitHub repository

LBNL database

06Build Order

Phased rollout over 8 weeks. Quick wins first, then the foundation engine, optimization layer, and integration.

Phase 1 Quick Wins Week 1-2
  • P4: Soft Sensor — debutanizer dataset, straightforward ML
  • P3: Predictive Maintenance — TEP dataset, classification and anomaly detection
  • P8: Market Intelligence — polish and connect existing engine
Phase 2 The Engine Week 2-4
  • P1: DWSIM CLI Engine — the hardest engineering (.NET extraction)
  • P7: Alarm Rationalization — derived from TEP data
  • P9: Real-Time Risk Dashboard — futures data + VaR computation
Phase 3 Optimization Week 4-6
  • P2: Crude Selection Optimizer — requires P1
  • P5: Blend Optimizer — requires P1
  • P10: APC Self-Tuning — requires P1 + P3 foundation
Phase 4 Integration Week 6-8
  • P6: Energy/Steam Optimizer — requires P1
  • P11: Demand Forecasting — PPAC data
  • P12: Digital Twin Dashboard — integration layer across all projects
